# AquaSense AI Demo Expo Pitch

## Project Summary

AquaSense AI is a predictive wastewater compliance monitoring platform. It helps treatment operators identify pollution risks before they become legal breaches by combining live sensor monitoring, short-term machine learning forecasts, explainable risk drivers, automated alerts, and compliance reporting.

The project is designed for wastewater treatment operators, food processing plants, environmental compliance teams, utilities, and regulators who need earlier warnings and clearer operational records.

## Stage 1 Demo Expo Pitch

### 1. Problem

Wastewater facilities often detect compliance problems too late.

Operators may only know there is a serious issue after pollutant levels have already exceeded legal discharge limits. At that point, the facility is reacting under pressure instead of preventing the problem. This can lead to environmental harm, regulatory penalties, costly emergency action, and time-consuming manual reporting.

The core problem is not only monitoring what is happening now. The bigger need is knowing what is likely to happen next, why it is happening, and what action should be taken before a breach occurs.

### 2. Solution

AquaSense AI moves wastewater compliance from reactive monitoring to predictive decision support.

The platform streams live wastewater sensor data, predicts short-term compliance risk, explains the main factors driving that risk, and turns the results into practical alerts and reports.

Instead of only saying, "a limit has been exceeded," AquaSense AI helps answer:

- What is the current status of the facility?
- Is there a likely compliance breach in the next 30 minutes?
- Which sensor readings are driving the risk?
- What action should operators consider?
- How can the incident be documented for compliance reporting?

### 3. Demo Walkthrough

#### Live Dashboard

The demo starts on the main dashboard. This page shows real-time wastewater readings such as:

- pH
- Chemical Oxygen Demand, or COD
- Total Suspended Solids, or TSS
- Biological Oxygen Demand, or BOD
- Ammonia
- Temperature
- Flow rate

The dashboard gives operators a quick view of current plant health and compliance status.

#### Predictive Risk

Next, the demo moves to the predictive risk page.

This page estimates the probability of a compliance breach in the next 30 minutes. It helps operators act before the facility crosses a limit, rather than waiting for a failure to happen.

The page also highlights the top risk drivers, so users can understand which parameters are contributing most to the warning.

#### Alerts

The alerts page shows how the system converts risk into operational action.

Alerts are grouped by severity and include recommended actions. This helps operators prioritize urgent issues and respond more consistently.

#### Reports

The reports page shows how AquaSense AI can generate compliance summaries.

This reduces manual reporting work and creates a clearer record of incidents, risk patterns, and operational decisions.

#### Data Quality

The data quality page checks sensor health and stream integrity.

This matters because predictions are only useful if the incoming data is reliable. The system helps identify missing, invalid, or anomalous readings.

#### Drought and Environmental Context

The drought page adds regional environmental context.

Drought, rainfall anomalies, and groundwater stress can affect wastewater operations, water availability, and regulatory planning. Including this context helps operators understand wider environmental pressure, not just internal plant readings.

### 4. Impact

AquaSense AI creates impact by helping facilities intervene earlier.

The platform can help:

- Reduce environmental harm by identifying risks before discharge limits are breached
- Avoid costly fines and compliance violations
- Support faster and better-informed operator decisions
- Reduce manual reporting workload
- Improve transparency for facility managers and regulators
- Build a clearer historical record of incidents and responses

The main value is turning wastewater data into early action.

### 5. What Is Next

The next stage would be to connect AquaSense AI to real plant sensor streams and expand the model with more site-specific data.

Future improvements include:

- Real-time integration with industrial sensor systems
- More site-specific model training and calibration
- SMS, email, or operator workflow notifications
- Stronger regulatory report exports
- Role-based access for operators, managers, and regulators
- Wider environmental datasets, including rainfall, drought, and river conditions

## Likely Judge Questions

### Who is this for?

AquaSense AI is for wastewater treatment operators, food processing plants, environmental compliance teams, utilities, and regulators.

### What problem does it solve?

It helps facilities detect and respond to compliance risks before legal limits are breached.

### Why use machine learning?

Machine learning allows the system to identify patterns across multiple sensor readings and estimate short-term breach risk, rather than relying only on fixed thresholds.

The project uses wastewater sensor readings, model-generated predictions, compliance thresholds, alert history, report data, and environmental context such as drought and rainfall stress.

### What is the real-world value?

The real-world value is earlier action. Operators can respond before an incident escalates, reduce environmental damage, avoid penalties, and spend less time manually preparing reports.

The next step is connecting the platform to real facility sensor streams, improving model calibration with site-specific data, and adding stronger notification and reporting workflows.
